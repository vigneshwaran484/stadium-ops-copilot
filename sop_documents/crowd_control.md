# Standard Operating Procedure: Crowd Control & Escalation

**SOP ID:** SOP-CROWD-001  
**Severity Level:** 🟠 HIGH  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-01  
**Applicable Zones:** All stadium zones, priority: gates, concourses, exits  

---

## Scope

This procedure covers crowd density management, bottleneck resolution, crowd surge prevention, gate flow management, and emergency crowd dispersal. Applies during ingress (entry), egress (exit), halftime, and any unscheduled crowd movement events.

## Crowd Density Awareness

| Level | Description | Density | Action |
|-------|-------------|---------|--------|
| 🟢 Green | Normal flow | < 2 persons/m² | Monitor |
| 🟡 Yellow | Elevated density | 2–4 persons/m² | Active management |
| 🟠 Orange | High density | 4–6 persons/m² | Intervene + report |
| 🔴 Red | Dangerous crush risk | > 6 persons/m² | EMERGENCY — escalate immediately |

## Immediate Response Steps

1. **Monitor crowd flow continuously** — Watch for signs of bottlenecks: fans stopping, crowd speed decreasing, fans pushing or being compressed.
2. **For Yellow level (elevated):**
   - Open additional lanes or pathways if available
   - Use hand signals and voice to direct fans: "Please keep moving, there's plenty of space ahead"
   - Radio Ch. 1: "Yellow crowd level at [location], managing flow"
3. **For Orange level (high density):**
   - Radio Ch. 1 IMMEDIATELY: "Orange crowd level at [location], requesting backup"
   - Begin redirecting fans to alternate routes
   - Deploy barrier tape or cones to create one-way flow if needed
   - Do NOT allow counter-flow (fans moving against the crowd direction)
   - Position yourself where you are visible and can be heard
4. **For Red level (dangerous):**
   - Radio Ch. 1 EMERGENCY: "Red crowd level at [location], CRUSH RISK, request immediate intervention"
   - DO NOT attempt to push or physically move fans
   - Open any available emergency exits in the area
   - Use PA system (request via Command Center) to redirect fans
   - Watch for fans falling — a fallen person in a dense crowd is a life-threatening emergency

## Gate Management

- **Ingress**: Stagger entry by opening gates in sequence. Monitor queue length — if queue extends beyond barrier line, radio for additional gate activation.
- **Egress**: Open ALL exit gates simultaneously. Never restrict exits after the event.
- **Re-entry**: Fans with valid tickets may re-enter through designated re-entry gates only (Gates B and F).

## ESCALATE IF

- Crowd density reaches **Orange or Red level**
- You observe **fans falling** or being **crushed against barriers**
- A **stampede or crowd surge** begins
- Fans are **climbing fences, barriers, or structures** to bypass congestion
- There is a **physical altercation** in a dense crowd area
- An **exit or gate is blocked** or non-functional during egress
- You are **unable to redirect crowd flow** with available personnel

## Important Notes

- **Your safety is priority** — If the crowd becomes uncontrollable, move to a safe elevated position and radio for help.
- **Never create a dead end** — Always ensure fans have a direction to move toward.
- During halftime, expect 40–60% of fans to move simultaneously. Pre-position at known bottleneck areas.
- Crowd crush kills through compressive asphyxiation — it can happen in under 60 seconds. Speed of escalation is critical.
