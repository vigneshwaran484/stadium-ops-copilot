# Standard Operating Procedure: Medical Emergency Response

**SOP ID:** SOP-MED-001  
**Severity Level:** 🔴 CRITICAL  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-01  
**Applicable Zones:** All stadium zones  

---

## Scope

This procedure covers all medical emergencies including cardiac events, breathing difficulties, seizures, severe allergic reactions, heat-related illness, falls with injury, and any situation where a person is unconscious, unresponsive, or in visible distress.

## Immediate Response Steps

1. **Assess the scene** — Ensure your own safety first. Do NOT move the patient unless there is immediate danger (fire, structural collapse).
2. **Call for medical support immediately** — Radio Channel 3 (Medical): "Medical emergency, [your zone], [brief description]." Example: "Medical emergency, North Stand Gate 7, male fan collapsed, unresponsive."
3. **Stay with the patient** — Keep the area clear of bystanders. Ask nearby volunteers to create a perimeter (3-meter radius minimum).
4. **Check responsiveness** — Tap shoulders, ask loudly: "Are you okay?" If NO response, proceed to step 5.
5. **Check breathing** — Look for chest rise for no more than 10 seconds. If NOT breathing or only gasping, begin CPR immediately.
6. **Start CPR if trained** — 30 chest compressions, 2 rescue breaths. Push hard and fast (100–120 compressions per minute, 5–6 cm depth).
7. **Request AED** — Radio: "Request AED to [location]." AED stations are located at every gate entrance and every concourse junction.
8. **Use AED when it arrives** — Follow the AED voice prompts. Do NOT stop CPR until the AED instructs you or medical team arrives.
9. **Document the incident** — Note the time of collapse, time CPR started, patient description, and any known medical history shared by companions.

## ESCALATE IF

- Patient is **unconscious or unresponsive**
- Patient is **not breathing** or has **no pulse**
- Patient is experiencing **chest pain** or suspected **cardiac arrest**
- Patient has a **severe allergic reaction** (anaphylaxis) — swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing
- Patient has suffered a **head injury** with loss of consciousness
- Patient is experiencing a **seizure** lasting more than 5 minutes
- There is **uncontrolled bleeding**
- Any situation involving a **child under 12** with medical distress

## Important Notes

- **NEVER administer medication** to any person, even if they request it.
- **Do NOT move** a person with a suspected spinal injury.
- If the patient is conscious, keep them calm and still. Offer water only if they are fully alert and not vomiting.
- All medical incidents must be logged in the Stadium Incident Reporting System within 30 minutes.
