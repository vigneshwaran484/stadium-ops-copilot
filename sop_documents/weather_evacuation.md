# Standard Operating Procedure: Weather Emergency & Evacuation

**SOP ID:** SOP-WX-001  
**Severity Level:** 🔴 CRITICAL  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-01  
**Applicable Zones:** All stadium zones  

---

## Scope

This procedure covers weather-related emergencies including lightning/thunderstorms, tornado warnings, extreme heat, high winds, flooding, and any weather event requiring fan shelter-in-place or full stadium evacuation.

## Weather Alert Levels

| Level | Condition | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 🟡 Watch | Severe weather possible within 60 min | Heightened awareness, monitor updates |
| 🟠 Warning | Severe weather imminent or occurring | Prepare for shelter/evacuation |
| 🔴 Emergency | Life-threatening conditions | Execute evacuation/shelter immediately |

## Lightning Protocol

1. **Lightning detected within 8 miles** → Match/event is paused
2. **PA announcement will be made**: "Attention guests, play has been temporarily suspended due to weather. Please remain in your seats under covered areas."
3. **Direct fans in open/uncovered sections** to the nearest covered concourse area
4. **Clear ALL outdoor exposed areas**: field-level seating, open upper decks, parking lots, tailgate areas
5. **30-minute rule**: Play resumes only after 30 minutes with no detected lightning within 8 miles

## Tornado Protocol

1. **Tornado Warning issued** → Command Center activates full shelter protocol
2. **PA announcement**: "Attention, a tornado warning has been issued. Please move calmly to the nearest interior shelter area immediately."
3. **Direct fans to interior shelter zones:**
   - Lower concourse restrooms and interior corridors
   - Below-grade areas (tunnels, loading docks if cleared by security)
   - **AVOID**: press boxes, upper deck open areas, glass-fronted suites, parking structures
4. **Instruct fans**: "Get low, cover your head, stay away from windows and exterior walls"
5. **Account for all volunteers in your zone** — radio check-in to Ch. 1

## Extreme Heat Protocol

1. **Heat index above 105°F (40.5°C)** → Activate cooling protocol
2. **Deploy hydration stations** at every gate and concourse junction
3. **Monitor fans for heat illness signs**: confusion, nausea, stopped sweating, loss of consciousness
4. **Heat exhaustion**: Move to shade, provide water, apply cold compresses → call Medical (Ch. 3) if no improvement in 10 minutes
5. **Heat stroke** (confusion, hot/dry skin, unconscious): **MEDICAL EMERGENCY** → follow SOP-MED-001 immediately

## Full Evacuation Procedure

1. **Evacuation order received** from Command Center via Radio Ch. 1
2. **PA system will guide primary evacuation** — supplement with your voice and hand signals
3. **Direct fans to the nearest EXIT** — do NOT allow fans to return to seats for belongings
4. **Assist persons with disabilities** — ensure wheelchair users and mobility-impaired fans are evacuated first via accessible routes
5. **Clear your assigned zone completely** — check restrooms, concession areas, and suites
6. **Report zone clear** to Command Center: "Zone [X] all clear, [number] fans evacuated via [exit]"
7. **Proceed to your designated rally point** after your zone is confirmed clear

## ESCALATE IF

- **Tornado warning** is issued for the stadium area
- **Lightning is detected** and fans refuse to leave exposed seating
- A fan shows signs of **heat stroke** (confusion, unconsciousness, hot/dry skin)
- **Evacuation route is blocked** or an exit is non-functional
- You are **unable to account for** volunteers in your zone during a weather event
- **Structural damage** is observed (leaks, ceiling damage, broken glass from wind)
- Fans are **panicking or stampeding** during weather evacuation

## Important Notes

- **You are the last to leave** your zone during evacuation — after confirming all fans and staff have exited.
- Know your zone's evacuation routes BEFORE your shift starts. Review the posted evacuation maps at your station.
- During lightning delays, fans may become frustrated. Calmly explain: "This is for everyone's safety. Play will resume as soon as conditions are safe."
- Keep your radio ON and volume UP during all weather events.
