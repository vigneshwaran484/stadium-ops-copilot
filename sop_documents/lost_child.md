# Standard Operating Procedure: Lost or Missing Child

**SOP ID:** SOP-CHILD-001  
**Severity Level:** 🔴 CRITICAL  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-01  
**Applicable Zones:** All stadium zones  

---

## Scope

This procedure covers all situations involving a lost, missing, or unaccompanied child (under 16 years of age) reported by a parent, guardian, or any stadium personnel. This also covers situations where a child appears distressed, alone, or is found without an identifiable guardian.

## Code Adam Protocol

"Code Adam" is the stadium's missing child alert protocol. When activated, all exits are monitored and a coordinated search begins.

## Immediate Response Steps

1. **Gather information immediately** — Ask the reporting parent/guardian:
   - Child's full name and age
   - Physical description: height, hair color, clothing (be specific: "blue FIFA jersey, khaki shorts")
   - Last known location and time last seen
   - Any medical conditions or special needs
   - Parent/guardian name and contact number
2. **Radio Command Center immediately** — Channel 2 (Security): "Code Adam, [zone], [child description]." Example: "Code Adam, East Concourse Section 204, 6-year-old girl, brown hair, red t-shirt, last seen 10 minutes ago."
3. **Stay with the reporting parent/guardian** — Do NOT leave them alone. Reassure them that the team is responding.
4. **If you FIND an unaccompanied child:**
   - Approach calmly and kneel to their eye level
   - Ask: "Hi, are you looking for your mom or dad? What's your name?"
   - Do NOT take the child to a different location — stay where you are
   - Radio: "Found unaccompanied child, [your location], [description]"
   - Keep the child in a visible, public area at all times
5. **Do NOT hand the child to anyone** claiming to be a parent without verification from Security or Command Center.
6. **Monitor all exits in your zone** — If Code Adam is active, politely inform fans that exit screening is temporarily in effect.

## ESCALATE IF

- Child has been **missing for more than 10 minutes**
- There is **any suspicion of abduction** or the child was seen leaving with an unknown adult
- The child is **under 5 years old**
- The child has a **medical condition** requiring medication or supervision
- The child is **visibly distressed, injured, or non-verbal**
- Parent/guardian suspects the child may have left the stadium

## Important Notes

- **NEVER leave a found child unattended** or hand them to unverified individuals.
- **Two-adult rule**: Always ensure at least two staff members are present when with an unaccompanied child.
- All missing child incidents must be logged immediately, even if resolved quickly.
- If the child speaks a different language, request a translator via Radio Ch. 1.
- Take a photo of the child's description details (clothing, features) to share with search teams if parent consents.
