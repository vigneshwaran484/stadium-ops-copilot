# Standard Operating Procedure: Accessibility & Wheelchair Assistance

**SOP ID:** SOP-ACCESS-001  
**Severity Level:** 🟡 MEDIUM  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-01  
**Applicable Zones:** All stadium zones  

---

## Scope

This procedure covers wheelchair assistance requests, accessible seating arrangements, mobility device support, service animal accommodations, and general accessibility needs for fans with disabilities or mobility impairments.

## Immediate Response Steps

1. **Greet the guest respectfully** — Speak directly to the person, not to their companion. Ask: "How can I assist you today?"
2. **Assess the need:**
   - **Wheelchair request**: Guest needs a stadium wheelchair for transport
   - **Accessible route guidance**: Guest needs directions to accessible entrances, elevators, or ramps
   - **Seating assistance**: Guest needs help reaching their accessible seating area
   - **Service animal**: Guest has a service animal and needs accommodations
   - **Sensory needs**: Guest requests a quiet room or sensory kit
3. **For wheelchair requests:**
   - Radio Channel 4 (Guest Services): "Wheelchair request, [location], [destination]"
   - Wheelchair fleet is stationed at: Gate A, Gate D, Gate G, and Guest Services Center
   - Estimated wait time: 5–10 minutes. Inform the guest.
   - Offer a nearby shaded/seated waiting area while the wheelchair arrives.
4. **For accessible route guidance:**
   - All gates have accessible entrances — look for the blue accessibility signs
   - Elevators are located at: Sections 101, 201, 301, and 401 (one per quadrant)
   - Ramp access is available at all main concourse levels
   - Accessible restrooms are located adjacent to every elevator bank
5. **For service animals:**
   - Service animals are permitted in all areas of the stadium
   - Do NOT ask about the nature of the disability
   - You MAY ask: "Is this a service animal required because of a disability?" and "What task has the animal been trained to perform?"
   - Provide a water bowl if requested (available at Guest Services)
6. **For sensory needs:**
   - Sensory quiet rooms are located at Sections 110 and 310
   - Sensory kits (noise-canceling headphones, fidget tools) available at Guest Services Center

## ESCALATE IF

- Guest reports their **accessible seating was given away** or is occupied
- Guest's **wheelchair or mobility device is damaged** at the stadium
- Guest is experiencing **a medical emergency** related to their disability (→ follow SOP-MED-001)
- Guest reports **discrimination or mistreatment** by staff or other fans
- Elevator or ramp is **out of service** and no alternative route is available

## Important Notes

- **Always ask before touching** a person's wheelchair or mobility device.
- **Do NOT push a wheelchair** without explicit permission from the occupant.
- Accessible seating companions must be seated adjacent to the accessible seat — never separated.
- If a guest's personal wheelchair needs charging, direct them to charging stations at Guest Services Center.
- Be patient and allow extra time. Never rush a guest with accessibility needs.
